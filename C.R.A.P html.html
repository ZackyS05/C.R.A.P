C.R.A.P


C.R.A.P stands for:


Contrast

Combining the different elements of art and an effective way of drawing attention.








Repetition

A recurrence of a particular line, pattern, shape, or other visual elements in a single or part of the series.








Alignment

Arranging different parts and aspects horzizontally or vertically.








Proximity

Group related items.


How I used C.R.A.P:

      Contrast:

      I used a light black background because it has some contrast in it. I then added gold color around the borders to make it look nice and to draw attention. I made the words of C.R.A.P big to show it's importance and to attract the reader's eyes.

      Repetition:

      I used repetition in this by adding the overline over the words of C.R.A.P. I also repeatedly added borders around the headings, paragraphs, and pictures to make the page look nice and neat.

      Alignment:

      I used alignment by arranging the images to the right and then to the left to make it look neat. I also aligned the text vertically right next to the image, so it does not look out of place.

      Proximity:

      I used proximity by grouping the headings and definitions to the corect image in order to tell the reader what it means. I also spaced them away from eachother and used lines to seperate them. I did that, so it would make it easier to read and to look at the different images and definitions.
